Median Income and Low-Income Percentage by Ward​:
This chart illustrates the stark income disparities across Toronto’s 25 wards. Median household income varies dramatically from just $31,400 in the lowest-income ward to over $100,000 in the highest. Alongside this, the percentage of low-income resident’s ranges from under 10% to over 22%. Notably, the wards with lower median incomes tend to have significantly higher low-income rates. This correlation highlights which communities are facing the greatest economic pressure and are most vulnerable to housing insecurity. It underscores the importance of directing affordable housing investment toward areas with the most financial need.
